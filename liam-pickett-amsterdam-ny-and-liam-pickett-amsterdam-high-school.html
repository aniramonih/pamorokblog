High school chemistry might not be the easiest or the most loved subject for many students. But there are a handful who consider it their favorite, largely because of a teacher who made all the difference. At Amsterdam High School, this was none other than Liam Pickett.

The school on Wednesday identified the 31-year-old chemistry teacher and drama coach who passed away suddenly following a medical emergency after his final class the previous day. The sudden grievous news had sent ripples of sadness through the Amsterdam High School community.

A Teacher Loved By All

Greater Amsterdam School District Superintendent, Richard Ruberti, mused over his memories of Pickett. "It's like the sun disappeared behind a cloud," he sighed. "Liam's warm smile and caring presence filled our hallways with a sense of belonging. That is a void that will be hard to fill."

He further added, "Our condolences go out to Mr. Pickett’s family during this incredibly challenging time. His smile and caring demeanor created a wonderful and welcoming atmosphere that will be missed by all students and staff.”

Remembering Liam Pickett isn't just about recollecting his classes, but also about how he could light up the room with his presence - quite like a lively chemical reaction, one might say.

The Fateful Tuesday

On the ill-fated Tuesday afternoon, Pickett had an apparent medical emergency immediately after concluding his last class for the day. School staff and emergency medical teams came to his aid as soon as possible. Despite their best efforts, Pickett passed away while receiving treatment at St. Mary’s Healthcare.

A Short But Noteworthy Tenure

Having only joined Amsterdam High School last year, Pickett's tenure may have been short, but the impact he's had is indescribably profound. His previous journey encompassed six wonderful years teaching chemistry and directing the theater program at Doane Stuart School in Rensselaer.

He held a special place in the hearts of everyone who knew him, demonstrating a veritable love for the job that was as apparent as chemical compounds under a microscope.

The School's Response

The greater community, including students' families, were informed of the tragedy through an initial notification late on Tuesday. Out of respect for his family, Pickett's identity was only disclosed on Wednesday.

Upon the sad news, Ruberti added, “The Greater Amsterdam School District expresses deep sorrow over the passing of an Amsterdam High School teacher,” an understatement for the pain the community is undergoing.

To help the school cope with the sudden loss, a three-hour delay was issued on Wednesday for the high school students, giving the staff time to gather and discuss the way forward.

At times such as these, emotional support becomes paramount to grieving students and staff alike. The district issued a statement, reassuring everyone that they were not alone. “Our team of school counselors, social workers and psychologists are ready to assist students and staff affected by this profound loss,” the statement read.

The Man Behind the Teacher

Apart from his obvious love for teaching, Pickett also held a passionate interest in drama. During his tenure at Doane Stuart, he transformed many introverts into extroverts with his innovative approach to directing the school's drama club.

Such was his charisma and ability to inspire, that in 2019, he was selected by WNYT NewsChannel 13 as a top teacher of the year. The station described him as an "inspiring" teacher who exerted a positive influence on his students and colleagues alike.

Honoring his love for drama, Liam also acted as the director and producer of the Footloose Theater Programs in Albany. This program grew out of a summer camp he started while working at Doane Stuart, further underlining his passion and commitment towards the arts.

His Legacy

Liam's influence extends beyond the confines of his chemistry classroom and the drama stage. The Niskayuna resident, a proud alumnus of Colonie Central High School and Marist College where he earned his bachelor’s degree in biochemistry, Pickett crafted a mission for himself: to be the educator that gives students the resources and guidance to explore every facet of the world.

To every student, colleague, and everyone who had the joy of knowing him, Liam Pickett was more than just a chemistry teacher. He was a mentor, a guide, and an everlasting source of inspiration. His story, his influence, and his legacy continue to resonate within the hearts of everyone in the Amsterdam High School community and beyond.
