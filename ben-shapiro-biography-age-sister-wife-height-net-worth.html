Early Life

Ben Shapiro was born on January 15, 1984 in Los Angeles, California, United States to a Jewish family. He is of mixed ethnicity of Russian-Jewish and Lithuanian-Jewish descent. His father, David Shapiro worked as a composer while his mother was an executive of a TV company.

He has a sister named Abigail Shapiro, who is an opera singer. He is also a cousin of Mara Wilson, a sensational writer and actress.

He attended Yeshiva University High School of Los Angeles in Westside, Los Angeles. He obtained a Bachelor of Arts degree in political science at the University of California, Los Angeles.

Career

Shapiro developed interest in politics at a very tender age and at the age of 17, he began a nationally syndicated column and had written two books at the age of 21.

Prior to fame, he worked at the law offices of Goodwin Procter. He later ran an independent consultancy firm, Benjamin Shapiro Consulting, in Los Angeles, in March 2012.

He became an editor-at-large of Breitbart News, in 2012 and resigned in 2016.

He co-founded TruthRevolt, an activism website, alongside business partner, Jeremy Boreing, on October 7, 2013.

Shapiro released his first and to date only fiction novel, True Allegiance in 2017 and in 2019, he published the book, The Right Side of History: How Reason and Moral Purpose Made the West Great. He followed up with another book, The Authoritarian Moment which he published in 2021.
