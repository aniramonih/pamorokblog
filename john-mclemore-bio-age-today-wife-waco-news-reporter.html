So, this is how John McLemore tells his perception of the American Apocalypse on the Netflix series.

At the time of the ATF raid, John McLemore was working as an investigative reporter and sports anchor for KWTX-TV in Waco, Texas. He was a pretty sought-after journalist and had notably covered the trial of the serial killer Kenneth McDuff just days before the raid happened. So, at this point, John was looking forward to a quiet weekend at home when his cameraman, Dan Mulloney, called him on the day of 28 February 1993 and informed him about the ATF operation that was about to transpire. Soon after, he and Dan drove down to the Mount Carmel Center compound outside Waco. The firing began of course and the situation soon escalated into a full-scale firefight.

John and Dan meanwhile focused entirely on the action for the entire raid. At last, when both sides called a ceasefire after 4 federal agents and 5 Branch Davidians were killed in it. John and his cameraman also helped transport some of the wounded in their car and were able to get the entire footage out to the station safely.

In the days that followed, John became kind of a celebrity. As an authentic eyewitness, he was invited to talk on several shows and was even nominated for an Emmy Award. But, that was short-lived as some news channels started to falsely accuse him of alerting the Branch Davidians about the raid. People thus began to hold him responsible as an accomplice for the murder of the federal agents. Things only got worse as he also lost his job, and there were none willing to recruit him.

Fast forward to 2018, when TexasMonthly reached out to him, he continued to remain wary of the rumors and false accusations that dogged him for the past 25 years. Yet, he said, he has no regrets.
