Biography

Angelos Postecoglou (born August 27, 1965), more commonly known as Ange Postecoglou, is an Australian football manager with an impressive background as a former player. Currently, he holds the prestigious manager position at Tottenham Hotspur in the Premier League.

Postecoglou showcased his skills on the field as a professional soccer player during his earlier career. His journey was primarily with South Melbourne Hellas, where he dedicated a significant portion of his playing days. In the late 1980s, he proudly represented Australia on four occasions. 

However, Ange Postecoglou‘s true prominence was when he transitioned to the manager role at South Melbourne Hellas in 1996. His managerial prowess was evident as he led the team to victory, securing the National Soccer League title twice and notably triumphing in the OFC Champions League in 1999.

Throughout his upbringing, Ange was blessed with loving and supportive parents named Jim Postecoglou and Voula Postecoglou, who nurtured him with care and guidance. 

Alongside his siblings, particularly his dear sister Liz Postecoglou, Ange Postecoglou experienced a joyous childhood filled with shared memories and adventures.

Career

Ange Postecoglou‘s journey in soccer started at a tender age where he showcased immense talent and swiftly climbed up the ladder of success with the South Melbourne Hellas, his hometown team. In 1984, he made his senior debut for South Melbourne and became an integral part of the club by playing a whopping 193 games.

During his time there, he achieved remarkable victories, securing two NSL championships in the 1984 and 1990–91 seasons. Additionally, Postecoglou proudly represented Australia at the U-17 and U-20 levels, further solidifying his skills on an international platform.

Ange Postecoglou‘s coaching career commenced in 1996 at South Melbourne, where he achieved success by clinching two NSL championships in 1997–98 and 1998–99. Furthermore, under his guidance, the club triumphed in the Oceania Club Championship in 1999, earning them a spot in the prestigious FIFA Club World Cup.

Postecoglou became the Australian U-20 team’s head coach in 2000. For seven years, he led the squad to the FIFA U-20 World Cups in 2001 and 2005. In 2007, Postecoglou relocated to Greece, where he assumed the head coach role for Panachaiki. After spending a season with the team, he returned to Australia in 2009 to take on the head coach position for Brisbane Roar.

Under Ange Postecoglou‘s leadership, Brisbane Roar achieved remarkable success by clinching the A-League Premiership in 2011 and securing the Championship title in 2011–12. Moreover, his outstanding managerial skills earned him recognition as the A-League Manager of the Year in 2010–11.

Postecoglou‘s appointment as the head coach of the Australian national team occurred in 2013. Under his guidance, the team succeeded by winning the 2014 FIFA World Cup and the 2015 AFC Asian Cup. His excellent coaching skills were acknowledged when he received the prestigious title of AFC Men’s Coach of the Year in 2015.

Postecoglou made his way to Japan in 2017, assuming the head coach role for Yokohama F. Marinos. Under his guidance, the team succeeded and secured the J1 League championship in 2019. More recently, in 2021, Postecoglou moved to Scotland, where he took over as the head coach of Celtic. 

He again exhibited his coaching prowess by leading Celtic to clinch the Scottish Premiership title during the 2021–22 season. In 2023, Postecoglou was appointed the head coach of Premier League club Tottenham Hotspur.

On October 6, 2023, Ange Postecoglou, the manager of Tottenham, is facing a potential dilemma regarding his midfield selection for the upcoming match against Wolves on Saturday. This predicament arises from the unfortunate injury suffered by James Maddison during their recent 4-1 loss against Chelsea.
