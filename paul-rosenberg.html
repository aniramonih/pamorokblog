Paul Rosenberg Biography

Paul Rosenberg is an American music manager, current CEO of Goliath Records, as well as a former President of Def Jam Recordings, famously known for his association with hip hop artist Eminem. He was born on August 1, 1971, in Detroit, Michigan, United States as Paul D. Rosenberg. Paul is also recognized for his associations with The Knux, DJ AM, Action Bronson, and pop-punk band Blink-182.

    Paul Rosenberg and Eminem

    In addition to being a manager of several kinds of music acts, Paul, along with Eminem, is also the co-founder of hip hop record label Shady Records, which was founded in the year 1999. Paul first became involved with Eminem in the year 1997, during the recording of the Slim Shady EP, and has since been featured in a skit (titled “Paul” except on The Eminem Show, where it was called “Paul Rosenberg”) on every album from The Slim Shady LP through Relapse, and Kamikaze, as a character concerned with the particular record, advising Eminem on steps to take as well as often ordering him to (tone down) the lyrics or scrap the album completely. With Eminem, Paul co-wrote every skit on the 2009 album Relapse.

    Paul Rosenberg Def Jam, Stepping Down From Def Jam

    On August 1, 2017, Paul was appointed Chairman and CEO of Def Jam Recordings, succeeding the current CEO, Steve Bartels. The transition took place in the year 2018. Paul has been a resident of Alpine, New Jersey, on February 21, 2020, Paul stepped down on his position as president in Def Jam and announced the formation of Goliath Records, a joint venture label with Universal Music Group.
