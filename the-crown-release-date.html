The Crown Release Date:- The Crown, the critically acclaimed Netflix series that chronicles the life and reign of Queen Elizabeth II, is coming to an end with its sixth and final season. The show, which has won numerous awards and captivated millions of viewers around the world, will conclude with two parts, the first one premiering on Thursday, November 16, 2023, and the second one on Thursday, December 14, 2023.

If you are a fan of The Crown, or if you are curious about the history and drama behind the British monarchy, you might be wondering what to expect from the last season. Here are some of the key details that you need to know before you binge-watch the final episodes of The Crown.

The Crown Release Date & Time

The first season of the series was released on 4 November 2016. The Crown covers almost six decades of Queen Elizabeth II’s reign, from her wedding in 1947 to the early 21st century. The fifth season, which was released on November 9, 2022, spanned from 1991 to 1997, during the premiership of John Major.

The sixth season will pick up from where the fifth season left off and will cover the events from 1997 to 2005, during the premierships of Tony Blair and Gordon Brown. Some of the major historical events that will be depicted in the final season include:

  • The death of Diana, Princess of Wales, in a car crash in Paris on August 31, 1997, and the aftermath of the tragedy, including the public mourning, the funeral, and the royal family’s response.
  • The Queen’s Golden Jubilee in 2002, marking the 50th anniversary of her accession to the throne, and the celebrations that took place across the Commonwealth.
  • The death of Queen Elizabeth The Queen Mother on March 30, 2002, at the age of 101, and the funeral that followed.
  • The death of Princess Margaret, Countess of Snowdon, on February 9, 2002, at the age of 71, and the funeral that followed.
  • The Iraq War, which began in 2003, and the controversy and protests that surrounded it, as well as the relationship between the Queen and the Prime Minister.
  • The wedding of Prince Charles and Camilla Parker Bowles on April 9, 2005, after decades of being in love but unable to marry due to their previous divorces and the opposition of the Church of England.

New Cast of The Crown

The Crown has been known for changing its principal cast every two seasons, to reflect the aging of the characters and the passage of time. The first and second seasons featured Claire Foy as Queen Elizabeth II, Matt Smith as Prince Philip, and Vanessa Kirby as Princess Margaret. The third and fourth seasons featured Olivia Colman as Queen Elizabeth II, Tobias Menzies as Prince Philip, and Helena Bonham Carter as Princess Margaret.

The fifth and sixth seasons feature a new ensemble of actors, who will portray the royal family in their later years. The main cast members are:

  • Imelda Staunton as Queen Elizabeth II, the sovereign of the United Kingdom and the Commonwealth realms.
  • Jonathan Pryce as Prince Philip, Duke of Edinburgh, the Queen’s husband and consort.
  • Lesley Manville as Princess Margaret, Countess of Snowdon, the Queen’s younger sister and a controversial figure in the royal family.
  • Dominic West as Charles, Prince of Wales, the Queen’s eldest son and the heir apparent to the throne.
  • Elizabeth Debicki as Diana, Princess of Wales, Charles’s first wife and the mother of Princes William and Harry.
  • Jonny Lee Miller as John Major, the Prime Minister of the United Kingdom from 1990 to 1997.
  • Olivia Williams as Camilla Parker Bowles, Charles’s long-time lover and second wife.
  • Claudia Harrison as Anne, Princess Royal, the Queen’s second child and only daughter.
  • Natascha McElhone as Penny Knatchbull, Lady Romsey, a close friend of Prince Philip and his first cousin once removed.
  • Marcia Warren as Queen Elizabeth The Queen Mother, the Queen’s mother and the widow of King George VI.

Guests Stars

  • Bertie Carvel as Tony Blair, the Prime Minister of the United Kingdom from 1997 to 2007.
  • Ed McVey as Gordon Brown, the Prime Minister of the United Kingdom from 2007 to 2010.
  • Meg Bellamy as Sarah Ferguson, Duchess of York, the former wife of Prince Andrew, Duke of York, and the mother of Princesses Beatrice and Eugenie.
  • Luther Ford as Prince William, Duke of Cambridge, the eldest son of Charles and Diana, and the second in line to the throne.
  • Salim Daw as King Abdullah II of Jordan, the monarch of Jordan and a friend of the Queen.
  • Khalid Abdalla as Dodi Fayed, the son of Egyptian billionaire Mohamed Al-Fayed, and Diana’s companion at the time of her death.

Episode Guide

Episode 1: “The People’s Princess” – The episode focuses on the death of Diana, Princess of Wales, in a car crash in Paris on August 31, 1997, and the aftermath of the tragedy, including the public mourning, the funeral, and the royal family’s response.

The episode also explores the relationship between the Queen and the new Prime Minister, and their differing views on the role of the monarchy and the modernization of the country.

Episode 3: “The Golden Jubilee” – The episode focuses on the Queen’s Golden Jubilee in 2002, marking the 50th anniversary of her accession to the throne, and the celebrations that took place across the Commonwealth. The episode also depicts the challenges and the joys that the Queen faced in her long reign, as well as the changes and the continuity that she witnessed in the world.

Episode 4: “The Last Farewell” – The episode focuses on the death of Queen Elizabeth The Queen Mother on March 30, 2002, at the age of 101, and the funeral that followed. The episode also pays tribute to the life and the legacy of the Queen Mother, who was a beloved and influential figure in the royal family and the nation.

Episode 5: “The Broken Crown” – The episode focuses on the death of Princess Margaret, Countess of Snowdon, on February 9, 2002, at the age of 71, and the funeral that followed. The episode also explores the complex and turbulent relationship between the Queen and her sister, who were very different in personality and lifestyle, but also very close and loyal to each other.

Episode 6: “The War of Words” – The episode focuses on the Iraq War, which began in 2003, and the controversy and protests that surrounded it, as well as the relationship between the Queen and the Prime Minister. The episode also shows the impact of the war on the royal family, especially on Prince Harry, who expressed his desire to serve in the army.

Episode 7: “The Happy Couple” – The episode focuses on the wedding of Prince Charles and Camilla Parker Bowles on April 9, 2005, after decades of being in love but unable to marry due to their previous divorces and the opposition of the Church of England.

Episode 8: “The Family Business” – The episode focuses on the role and the responsibilities of the Prince of Wales, as the heir apparent to the throne and the leader of the Duchy of Cornwall, a vast estate that provides income and influence to the royal family.

Episode 9: “The Queen’s Speech” – The episode focuses on the Queen’s annual Christmas broadcast, which is a tradition that dates back to 1932, when her grandfather, King George V, delivered the first radio message to the Empire.

Episode 10: “The Crown” – The final episode of the series, which is also the title of the show, is a retrospective and a tribute to the Queen and her reign, as well as to the show and its creators.
