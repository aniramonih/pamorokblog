John McCook Addresses Future With Show

 Posted on August 12, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  414 words  |  Martina Birk

The Bold and the Beautiful (B&B) spoilers revealed that John McCook addressed his future on the show. He teased his retirement plans after his character Eric Forrester had fans wondering about his fate. Read on to learn more.

B&B Spoilers: John McCook Talks Retirement

According to The Bold and the Beautiful spoilers, fans were worried about Eric’s health. At the end of 2023, things took a dire turn. Eric almost went to the pearly gates. Luckily, he made it through the medical experiment and got a second lease on life.

In an interview with Soaps.com, John McCook talked about his retirement plans. He joked that he will continue “working right up until they drag me out of here.” McCook feels lucky to have such a long-spanning career. He’s been a part of the show since its debut in 1987. McCook has no plans to retire anytime soon because he loves what he does.

“It’s fun, it’s creative, I like the people that I work with,” John McCook said. “After my lovely wife and wonderful children, it’s the next thing that I’m grateful for — this job and the work that I’m fortunate enough to get to do.”

According to The Bold and the Beautiful spoilers, he intends on working until the day “they’re going to have to call my wife and say, ‘Come get him!’ because until I can no longer remember lines or have some kind of physical limitation, I have no reason to stop working!”

The Bold And The Beautiful Spoilers: John McCook Excited About The Future

According to The Bold and the Beautiful spoilers, John McCook is excited about his future on the show. He arrives at work, excited to see what else will happen to his character. McCook is still actively engaged with Eric Forrester’s storyline and ensures there is plenty of story to tell.

“Every time I get something new to play, I’m thrilled,” McCook continued. “Because how many old-man stories are there? I have to rely on Brad [Bell] and the writers to keep coming up with things, because I don’t want to be the old guy, bouncing his grandchildren on his knee and saying sweet things to them.”
