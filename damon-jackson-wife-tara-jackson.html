Damon Jackson: Ultimate Fighting Championship

Jackson fought Yancy Medeiros at UFC 177 on August 30, 2014, after having to step in at the last minute as a replacement for an injured Justin Edwards.

Jackson suffered his first defeat of his professional career in this fight, which ended in the second round via submission.

At UFC Fight Night 67, which took place on May 30, 2015, Jackson fought Rony Jason. In the first round, his opponent submitted him with a triangle choke, and he was defeated.

Because Jason had tested positive for hydrochlorothiazide, which is a restricted diuretic, on June 18, 2015, it was reported that his submission victory against Jackson had been reversed, and he earned a nine-month suspension as a result.

On January 30, 2016, Jackson competed against Levan Makashvili at UFC on Fox 18 in Las Vegas. Makashvili was docked one point in the third round for an illegal knee and eye poke, but the judges still scored the fight a draw (28-28, 28-28, and 29-27).

Jackson defeated Nate Jennerman at Legacy Fighting Alliance 47 on August 10, 2018, and won the Featherweight Interim Legacy Fighting Alliance championship by way of a knockout in round two.

This victory came after Jackson had a record of 6-1 while competing under the banner of the Legacy Fighting Alliance (LFA).

He would sign with the PFL to fight Movlid Khaybulaev on May 23, 2019, at PFL 2, however he would be defeated by a flying knee just 10 seconds into the fight.

After suffering the defeat, Jackson competed again in the LFA at event number LFA 83 and won by submission with a rear-naked choke in the first round against Mauro Chaulet.

On March 12, 2022, UFC Fight Night 203 was supposed to be when Jackson was supposed to fight Joshua Culibao. Culibao was scheduled to participate in the event; but, for reasons that have not been made public, he was substituted by Kamuela Kirk.

Jackson won the bout after forcing a submission in the second round with an arm triangle.

The fight between Jackson and Darrick Minner was set to take place at UFC Fight Night 207 on June 4, 2022. Minner was scheduled to compete in the event; however, for reasons that have not been made public, he was substituted by a newcomer named Daniel Argueta.
