The Journey Begins

Eleanor Morton, a Scottish powerhouse of comedy, embarked on her comedic journey at the tender age of 18, lighting up stages with her unique blend of stand-up and sketch comedy.

With a foundation in English and Scottish Literature from the University of Glasgow, complemented by screenwriting studies at the Met Film School, Eleanor’s academic background has undoubtedly enriched her comedic storytelling.

Rising Through the Ranks

Morton’s early career saw her diving headfirst into the comedy scene, with her debut at the Edinburgh Fringe in 2014 with Lollipop, a show that not only showcased her talent but also earned her a nomination for a Scottish Comedy Award.

FAQs about Eleanor Morton

Who is Eleanor Morton?

She is a Scottish comedian, actor, and writer known for her stand-up comedy, viral videos, and contributions to various comedy shows and festivals.

What is Eleanor Morton’s nationality?

She is Scottish.

Where did Morton study?

She studied English and Scottish Literature at the University of Glasgow and screenwriting at the Met Film School.

When did Eleanor start her comedy career?

She did stand-up comedy while at university, with her first gig in 2010. She reached the semi-finals of the Chortle Student Award in 2013.

What was Eleanor’s Edinburgh Fringe debut?

She made her Edinburgh Fringe debut in 2014 with her show titled ‘Lollipop’.

How did Morton gain a wave of new fans?

During the Covid lockdowns, Morton began creating viral videos in the character of Craig, a jaded guide giving tours around cultural sites, which resulted in a wave of new fans.

What are some of Morton’s stand-up shows?

Some of her shows include ‘Lollipop’ (2014), ‘Allotted Mucking Around Time’ (2015), ‘Happy Birthday Katie Lewis!’ (2016), ‘Angry Young Woman’ (2017), ‘Great Title, Glamorous Photo’ (2018), ‘Post-Morton’ (2019), and ‘Eleanor Morton Has Peaked’ (2022).

What radio show did Morton co-write and co-star in?

Is she involved with The Alternative Comedy Memorial Society?
