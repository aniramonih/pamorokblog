About Ramone's Parents' Relationship Journey

Ramone's parents Rocko and Monica were previously romantic love birds and dated each other for more than a decade. However, their relationship sadly ended in early 2010. They first met in early 2000 and soon began dating after knowing each other for some time.

After four years of dating relationship, they got separated from each other in 2004 due to some personal reasons, soon to be reunited together after a few months. On May 21, 2005, Monoco gave birth to their first child, Rodney Ramone Hill III, named after Rocko's father name. 

On December 24, 2007, on the special occasion of Christmas Eve, the couple engaged each other. The former couple welcomed their second child on January 8, 2008, and gave him a beautiful name, Romelo Montez Hill, which means citizen of Rome.

His Father Singing Career

Rodney Ramone Hill Jr, aka Rocko, started his professional career as a ghostwriter & producer of songs in 1999. He struggled in his music career for some years & did not get much attention from the public in his initial career stage. He got a breakthrough from his hard time after establishing his label Rocky Road Records. 

He started collaborating with rappers and singers like AL Young Dro and Dem Franchize Boyz. In 2006, Rodney was signed by L.A. Reid to work for Def Jam Records. He released several songs while working with Def Jam Records. 

In 2008, he released his first studio album, Self Made. This album consists of fourteen songs, including Hustle Fo, Busy; Umma Do Me, etc. Moreover, his song Umma Do Me was able to secure a position on Billboard Hot 100 list.

Who Is Monica?

As previously mentioned, Monica Denise Arnold is an American singer and rapper who has been in the limelight for her various songs. Some of her famous songs are Everytime Tha Beat Drop, The Boy Is Mine, Why I Love You So Much, and Before You Walk out of My Life.

Monica was born on October 24, 1980, in College Park, Georgia, United States, as the only daughter of Marilyn Best and M.C. Arnold Jr. Her mother was a renowned singer of her time, whereas her father was a mechanic. 

Monica was greatly influenced by her mother's singing career and had a dream of becoming a singer during her childhood days. She not only fulfilled her dream of becoming a singer but also became a rapper and an actress. 

She started her singing career at the early age of eleven and started working in Arista Records-distributed label Rowdy Records after being discovered by Dallas Austin.
