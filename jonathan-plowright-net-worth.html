FAQs about Jonathan Plowright

Who is Jonathan Plowright?

He is an English classical pianist renowned for his performances and recordings, particularly of Romantic and neglected Polish composers.

When and where was Jonathan Plowright born?

He was born in 1959 in Yorkshire, England.

What is Plowright known for?

Plowright is known for championing neglected music from Polish Romantic composers and has made significant contributions to the recording of such works.

Where did Plowright study?

He studied at Stonyhurst College, the Royal Academy of Music in London, and later at the Peabody Conservatory in Baltimore under a Fulbright Scholarship.

What awards has Plowright won?

Among others, he has won the Baltimore Symphony Orchestra Award (1984) and the European Piano Competition (1989).

What are some notable performances or premieres by Plowright?

He gave the world premiere of Constant Lambert’s Piano Concerto and has performed at major venues like Carnegie Hall and Wigmore Hall.

What recordings is Plowright known for?

He is noted for his recordings of the complete solo piano works of Johannes Brahms and works by various Polish composers on labels like Hyperion and BIS Records.

Has Plowright taught music?

Yes, he taught part-time at the Royal Conservatoire of Scotland from 2007 until 2020 and frequently gives masterclasses worldwide.

What recent projects or recordings has Plowright done?

His recordings include works like Brahms’s solo piano works and various Polish compositions.

What unique musical interests does Plowright have?

He has a particular interest in reviving works by lesser-known composers and has contributed to the rediscovery of several Polish and other Romantic composers.
