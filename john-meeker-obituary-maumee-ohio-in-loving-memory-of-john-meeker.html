John Meeker Obituary, Death – After a long and courageous fight against Parkinson’s disease that lasted seven years, John F. “Bud” Meeker, 86, of Londonderry, New Hampshire, left this life on Tuesday, October 17, 2023, at his home, surrounded by his family. On April 24, 1937, he was brought into the world by his parents, John W. Meeker and Evangeline (Perry). Juanita (Murray) Meeker, who is still alive, was his high school sweetheart, and he eventually married her.

On May 18th, 2023, they celebrated their 65th wedding anniversary with family and friends. He was blessed with two daughters and their husbands, Cheryl and Kevin Hill and Jody and Dan Clary; one son and his wife, Jeff and Becky Meeker; seven grandchildren, Christy (Jayson) Howard, Lisa (Brad) Montgomery, Amy (Andy) Smith, Scott (Kelli) Clary, John (Patricia) Clary, Ross (Kaitlyn) Meeker and Lucas (Ashley) Meeker; and his great-grandchildren, Mason Montgomery His sister, Sue Kirk, and his brother-in-law, Joe Fisher, both made it through his passing unscathed.

His parents, his sister Martha Fisher, and his brother-in-law Bruce Kirk all passed away before him. Bud was grateful for the help he received from his friends Buzz, Bobbin, and Gary. He worked the land in the vicinity of Londonderry for more than half a century. In addition to having worked for Mead Paper for many years before retiring, Bud was the owner of Buffalo Trail Campground and served as the clerk of Liberty Township for a total of 12 years. He had been a member of Scioto Lodge #6, F&AM for the past half a century and was a former member of the Ross County Farmers Club.
