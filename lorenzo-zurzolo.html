Career and Professional Life

Talking about his social media presence, Lorenzo is a young actor. He gained fame after playing the role of Niccolo Govender Rossi in the 2018 Italian TV series Baby.

Likewise, people also recognize this young actor for his recurring role in Questo e il mio Paese. It is a 2015 series where Zurzolo played the role of Nino Ferrari.

Recently in 2020, he played the role of a blind teenager in Sotto il sole di Riccione (Under the Riccione Sun).

The same year, he also appeared as Alessandro in the psychological thriller Weekend. Also, in 2021, he appeared in the Italian film, Morrison.

The young actor has bagged a few prestigious awards at such a young age.

He received the Leopoldo Trieste Prize at the prestigious Reggio Calabria Film Festival for his role in the 2017 film Sconnessi. Likewise, he won the Magna Grecia Awards for Best acting revelation in 2020.

The same year, he also received the Explosive talent award that is given to emerging Italian artists at the 2020 Giffoni film festival.

Additionally, he received a special prize called Astro Nascente Italian Cinema in the reputed Golden Graal Award of Italy.

More Details

Talking about the beginning of his career, this young actor started his acting career in the year 2007.

At the young age of 7 years, this actor landed a small role in the series Decameron di Daniele Luttazzi. In this series, the actor played the role of Dirigente Rai.

Later on, the young actor made his feature film debut in the year 2012. This was when the actor played the role of Ruolo Minore in Young Europe.

For this project, the actor shared the screen with the Italian actress Maria Luisa de Crescenzo. He also appeared as Michele in Una Pallotta nel Cuore (Bulletproof Heart) in 2014.

Prior to all his fame, he also worked on projects like Outing – Fidanzati per sbaglio in 2013 and Don Matteo in 2000.

This actor loved Japanese anime and superhero movies as a child. Among his favorites included action genres like Dragon Ball.

Furthermore, the actor watched Kill Bill at age 12. The young actor was fascinated by that movie and its director – Quentin Tarantino.

The actor names directors Roberto Benigni, Wes Anderson, and David Lynch as his early idols.
