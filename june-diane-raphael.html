    June Diane Raphael Facts

  • She has done extensive activism work for Everytown for Gun Safety organization, which aims to promote gun control in the United States.
  • At the Upright Citizens Brigade Theater, she started performing a 2-woman sketch show with Casey Wilson, who was her best friend in college.
  • After watching June and Wilson perform at the U.S. Comedy Arts Festival, the New Regency Pictures asked them to write the movie, Bride Wars, in 2005.
  • Along with writing the first screenplay of the movie, June and Casey were also cast in the supporting roles in the movie, Bride Wars. 
  • Her performance at the U.S. Comedy Arts Festival with Casey Wilson also helped her bag a development deal to create a sitcom pilot from UPN.
  • In 2010, she wrote a couple of short stories for the book, Worst Laid Plans.
  • She has collaborated with comedian Kulap Vilaysack to establish UCB Corps, which is associated with the Upright Citizens Brigade Theater and is a voluntary charity organization. She also serves as the co-director of the organization.
  • She has collaborated with her husband Paul Scheer and comedian Jason Mantzoukas to host podcast How Did This Get Made? in which they discuss popular movies.
