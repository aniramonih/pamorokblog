Aaron Rodgers NFL Career 

Initially serving as Brett Favre‘s backup for the first three years of his NFL career, Rodgers patiently waited for his opportunity to shine.

In 2008, he finally became the Packers’ starting quarterback and quickly made a name for himself.

In the 2010 season, Rodgers led the Packers to victory in Super Bowl XLV against the Pittsburgh Steelers, earning the prestigious Super Bowl MVP title.

Rodgers’ success continued to soar, earning him numerous accolades throughout his career.

In 2011, he was named Associated Press Athlete of the Year, and the Associated Press has voted him league MVP for 2011, 2014, 2020, and 2021 NFL seasons.
