Danny Huston’s Background

Danny Huston, born on May 14, 1962, is a multi-talented American actor, writer, and director known for his diverse career. He started his journey in the entertainment industry by directing “Mr. North,” which featured Anthony Edwards, Robert Mitchum, and his sister, Anjelica Huston. His acting breakthrough came in the independent film “Ivans Xtc,” for which he received a nomination for Best Male Performance at the Independent Spirit Awards in 2003.

His impressive filmography includes roles in notable movies such as “Birth,” “The Aviator,” “The Constant Gardener,” “Marie Antoinette,” “The Kingdom,” “Robin Hood,” “Hitchcock,” and “The Congress,” which was showcased at the Cannes Film Festival in 2013. Additionally, he has been part of television series like “American Horror Story: Coven.”
