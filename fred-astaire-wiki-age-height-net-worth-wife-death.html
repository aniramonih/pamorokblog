Fred Astaire’s Age & Early Life

Fred Astaire was born on May 10, 1899, in Omaha, Nebraska, to Frederic Austerlitz and Johanna Austerlitz. He had a younger sister, Adele Astaire. He had a mixed heritage of Ashkenazi Jewish-German and held American nationality. He was born under the Taurus astrological sign. He received his early education at Alvienne School of Dance in New York and Ned Wayburn Studio of Stage Dancing.

Fred Astaire Height & Weight

Fred Astaire stood at 5 feet 9 inches and weighed 61 kg. He had brown hair and eyes.

Fred Astaire’s Net Worth

How much is Fred Astaire’s net worth? His exact earnings are not available, but his net worth was estimated at $10 million.

Career

His career began early, performing with his sister Adele. Despite initial success, he initially struggled to break into the film industry. However, he eventually gained attention and landed a part in Joan Crawford’s 1933 film Dancing Lady. Subsequently, he starred with Ginger Rogers in the 1933 film Flying Down to Rio, which launched a successful partnership. Their performances showcased a mix of dance styles including tap, ballroom, and ballet. His on-screen chemistry with Rogers garnered widespread acclaim.

He continued to work with other prominent performers such as Rita Hayworth, Cyd Charisse, Judy Garland, Leslie Caron, and Audrey Hepburn. As his film appearances decreased, he turned his focus to television and also took on dramatic roles. Notably, he collaborated with Gene Kelly on the documentary That’s Entertainment, which celebrated the golden era of the film musical.

Death

Fred passed away on June 22, 1987, in Los Angeles, California, at the age of 88 due to pneumonia.

Fred Astaire Wife & Marriage

Who is Fred Astaire’s wife? Fred Astaire was married twice. At the age of 81, he married Robyn Smith in 1980. His first marriage was to Phyllis Potter in 1933, with whom he had two children: Fred Astaire Jr., born January 1, 1936, and Ava Astaire-McKenzie, born March 19, 1942. Phyllis Potter passed away from lung cancer in 1954.
