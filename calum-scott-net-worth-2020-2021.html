Career

After quitting his job, Calum formed the electronic duo, The Experiment with John McIntyre. The debut single, “Girl (You’re Beautiful),” was released on 14 June. John and Calum performed the song on Good Morning Britain and BBC Look North. Later the duo parted ways.

Calum and his sister Jade auditioned for the ninth series of Britain’s Got Talent in 2015. But Jade did not qualify the auditions. When it was his turn, Calum performed a cover of Robyn’s “Dancing On My Own,” the performance was met with a standing ovation from the judges. Judge Cowell, in fact, pressed the Golden Buzzer, guaranteeing Scott, a place in the live shows. Cowell said of Scott’s singing: “I’ve never ever in all the years I’ve done this show heard a guy with the talent you’ve got. Seriously. And the version was sensational, and that shows to me that you’re more than a singer, you’re an artist.”

Soon after this audition, Calum’s Twitter following went from 400 to 25,000. The video of the audition itself was viewed over 262,000,000 times on YouTub. Calum became a household name and also got support from celebrities like Little Mix and Ashton Kutcher. Scott made it all the way to the semi-finals where he performed “We Don’t Have to Take Our Clothes Off” by Jermaine Stewart, which fetched him 25.6% of the votes, sending him straight to the finals. In the finals, he performed Rihanna’s “Diamonds,” but his performance got him 8.2% of the votes, and he finished 6th among the 12 finalists. BGT changed Scott’s career graph entirely.

Calum traversed the entire length of the United Kingdom performing in shows, including Viking FM Future Star Awards, Flamingo Land Resort Fair, Westwood Cross Shopping Centre’s tenth anniversary, Gibraltar Summer Nights, Hull Daily Mail’s Star, and Dartford Festival. Exactly a year later, he released his cover of “Dancing On My Own” independently.

It became a sleeper hit, first hitting number 40 in the charts in May and climbing into the top 40. On 5 August, it hit number two on the UK Singles Chart. It sold over 600,000 copies in the UK. The same year he released the promotional single “Transformar” with Brazilian recording artist Ivete Sangalo as the official theme of the 2016 Summer Paralympics Games. The duo performed the song at the closing ceremony of the Paralympics on 18 September.

Scott announced on Twitter on 24 May 2016 that he had signed a record deal with Capitol Records for his debut album Only Human. In 2017 he started working on his debut album, which finally released in 2018.  He also toured the US in 2017 and released the single “You Are the Reason,” which was later revised and released in collaboration with Leona Lewis, and performed by the two on The One Show in February 2018. “What I Miss Most” was the fourth single from Only Human, which was released in May 2018.

In October 2018, Scott released a new single, which spoke of his personal journey as a member of the LGBTQ community, his experience of coming out in the open. The single called “No Matter What” is, according to Scott, is the best performance so far as the song was so close to his heart.
