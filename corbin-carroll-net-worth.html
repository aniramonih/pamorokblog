When Did Corbin Carroll Make His Professional Debut?

Carroll was chosen by the Arizona Diamondbacks with the 16th overall pick. He made his professional debut with the Arizona League Diamondbacks after signing a $3.7 million contract. On August 8, he was promoted to the Hillsboro Hops after hitting .291 over 31 games with two home runs, 14 RBIs and 16 stolen bases.

Carroll missed all of 2020’s minor league games because the COVID-19 epidemic forced the minor league season to be canceled. Carroll started the 2021 season back in Hillsboro. Carroll, however, suffered a season-ending shoulder surgery after hurting his shoulder on a home run in early May.

Carroll and the Amarillo Sod Poodles kicked off the 2022 season. He was traded to the Reno Aces in early July. He batted for three minor league teams in 2022. On August 29, 2022, the Diamondbacks promoted Carroll to the major leagues.

Later that day, against the Philadelphia Phillies, he made his debut. At 30.7 feet per second, he was the fastest sprinter among all major league players. Carroll and the Diamondbacks agreed to an eight-year, $111 million contract deal on March 11, 2023.

Carroll will start as an outfielder in the 2023 All-Star Game, which will be his first All-Star participation in his career, it was revealed on June 29, 2023. Carroll made Major League history on September 20, 2023, when he became the first rookie to ever record 50 stolen bases and 25 home runs in a single season.

Carroll became the second-youngest player in MLB history with at least three hits and two thefts in a playoff game in Game 7 of the 2023 National League Championship Series, only behind Ty Cobb, who did so in the 1908 World Series at the age of 21.
