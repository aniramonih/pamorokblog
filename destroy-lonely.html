Bobby Wardel Sandimanie III widely recognized by his stage name “Destroy Lonely” is a talented American rapper like Autumn(TwinUzis) who gained significant recognition with the release of his mixtape titled “No Stylist” in 2022.

The mixtape earned him a spot on the prestigious Billboard 200 chart, peaking at number 91. It is worth noting that Destroy Lonely comes from a musical lineage, being the son of rapper I-20, who was signed to the renowned label Disturbing tha Peace, founded by Ludacris.

Profile Summary

Early Life & Background

Bobby Wardel Sandimanie III known by his stage name Destroy Lonely was born on July 30, 2001, in Atlanta, Georgia. He grew up with two younger sisters and an older half-brother, although he often felt more like an only child due to the age difference.

In his early years, Lonely embarked on a unique educational path. In the sixth grade, he began homeschooling with the permission of his grandmother, who happened to be a former teacher.

This alternative approach to education allowed him the freedom to explore his creativity and dedicate more time to his musical pursuits.

However, during his time in the ninth and tenth grades, Lonely faced personal struggles, including a battle with substance abuse.

He openly acknowledges abusing the drug Xanax during this period, which he felt was taking a destructive toll on his life.

It was during this challenging time that he found inspiration for the “Destroy” part of his stage name, symbolizing his desire to overcome and leave behind the negative aspects of his life.

Furthermore, his adolescent years were characterized by a sense of solitude, which he attributes to the “Lonely” part of his stage name. This period of isolation provided him with the opportunity for introspection and self-discovery.

Professional Career

Destroy Lonely embarked on his musical journey at the young age of 14, where he began creating music and recording his first songs in a recording studio at his school.

During this time, he had the opportunity to collaborate with two of his closest friends, Texaco and Nezzus, who played significant roles in producing his early works. Their collaboration continued on future projects throughout their time in school.

After forming a connection with Nezzus, the duo wasted no time and immediately started working on their first joint project titled “NezzusDestroyed.”

This marked the beginning of their creative partnership, laying the foundation for their future endeavors. Lonely’s breakthrough came in 2019 with the release of his track “Bane.”

The song garnered attention and started gaining traction, helping to establish his presence in the music scene. With this early success, he began to build a following and set himself on a promising path in his music career.

Mixtape & Record Label Signing

On September 25, 2020, Lonely released his third mixtape titled “</3” (also known as “Broken Hearts”), followed by the deluxe edition “</3²” on October 31, 2020. Notably, his talent caught the attention of Playboi Carti in December 2020 after the release of the music video for his song “Oh Yeah.”

In early 2021, Destroy Lonely signed to Playboi Carti’s record label “Opium” marking a significant milestone in his career. He appeared in Carti’s music video for the song “Sky” in April 2021.

The same year, he was announced as a performer at the Lyrical Lemonade Summer Smash, a notable music event. He continued his collaborative efforts and was featured on Ken Carson’s album “X” in July 2022. Additionally, he appeared on Bktherula’s single “Forever Pt. 2” and Slump6s‘ album “Genesis.”

In August 2022, he released his fifth mixtape titled “No Stylist,” which showcased his artistic growth. The mixtape features a collaboration with labelmate Ken Carson.

The following month, Lonely and Ken Carson gifted Playboi Carti a ring for his 26th birthday, highlighting their camaraderie within Opium. In October 2022, he announced the dates for his “No Stylist” tour, indicating his plans for live performances.

Furthermore, in October 2022, Lonely released a music video for his track “VTMNTSCOAT.” The video is described as featuring imagery of elaborate dinner parties and midnight car rides, adding visual depth to his music.

On November 18, 2022, he released a deluxe version of his mixtape “No Stylist” titled “NS+ (Ultra).”

Music Videos & Album “IF Looks Could Kill”

Following this, on February 20, 2023, he premiered the music video for his songs “NEVEREVER” and “FAKENGGAS” on his YouTube channel.

On February 21, 2023, Lonely took to Tumblr to announce that his next album would be released in April 2023, building anticipation among his followers. The announcement generated excitement for the upcoming project and sparked curiosity about the direction of his music.

On March 3, 2023, he released the highly anticipated single “If Looks Could Kill.” This track was expected to serve as the lead single for his debut studio album, showcasing his growth as an artist and setting the stage for the forthcoming release.

Finally, on May 5, 2023, he unveiled his highly anticipated album, “If Looks Could Kill.” The album consists of 25 tracks, including a bonus track featuring his labelmate, Ken Carson. Additionally, two additional tracks were exclusively released with the CD and vinyl versions of the album.
