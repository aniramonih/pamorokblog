Abel Tesfaye Ethnicity: Is He Ethiopian?

Abel Tesfaye, widely known by his stage name “The Weeknd,” is indeed of Ethiopian descent.

Born to Ethiopian immigrant parents, his Ethiopian heritage significantly shapes his identity and music.

As he grew up, Tesfaye spoke Amharic, the official language of Ethiopia, with his grandmother and mother.

He also attended Ethiopian Orthodox church services, further connecting him to his Ethiopian roots.

His Ethiopian background, Amharic language, and culture have left a lasting impact on his music.

In some of his songs, he includes Amharic lyrics, showcasing his pride in his heritage.

While Tesfaye is Canadian, he embraces and acknowledges his Ethiopian ethnicity, often paying tribute to it through his music.

This cultural background has undoubtedly contributed to the unique and diverse style that has pushed him to gain global fame.

Meet Abel Tesfaye Parents

Abel Tesfaye was born to Ethiopian parents, Makkonen Tesfaye and Samrawit Hailu who came to Canada in the late 1980s.

His mother, Samrawit Hailu, and his father, Makkonen Tesfaye, raised Abel in the Scarborough area of Toronto.

Although his parents eventually split, Abel was primarily raised by his mother and grandmother. They provided a supportive and nurturing environment for Abel.

Despite the lack of a close relationship, Abel has expressed that he holds no ill feelings toward his father.

While Abel’s relationship with his parents may not have been conventional, their influence and support played a vital role in shaping him into the successful artist he is today.
